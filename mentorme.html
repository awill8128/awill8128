Background

Problem Statement

Many individuals, particularly those from lower-income groups or with limited education, often experience heightened anxiety and hesitation when it comes to seeking expert advice through in-person interactions. This anxiety is a significant barrier to accessing the guidance and knowledge they require to find the guidance needed to succeed in a mentorship.

Research Goals

  • Explore the user’s preferences, preferred communication methods, frequency of interaction, and mentorship styles (e.g., coaching, advising, hands-on guidance) from both mentors and mentees.
  • Explore potential pain points faced by mentors and mentees during their mentorship journey and identify effective solutions to address them.
  • Identify the key information needed for user’s to find the right mentor.

I conducted user interviews where participants answered questions regarding their behaviors/attitudes, frustration, and needs/goals from using a mentorship application.  Check out the user interviews and questions here.

Sitemap Refinement: Enhancing User Journeys

conducted a hybrid card sorting exercise to streamline our original sitemap and potentially introduce new pages. Here's what we aimed for and what I discovered:

  • Strong consensus on categories like "Resources", "Mentors", and "User Profiles".
  • Varying agreement levels observed in categories such as "Calendar" and "Messages"
  • Insights from the exercise led to the addition of features like "goal tracking" and "notifications".

Following the card sorting exercise, I refined the sitemap to better align with user preferences and navigation patterns. This optimization ensured a more intuitive and user-friendly structure, enhancing the overall usability of the platform.

Elevating with Prototyping and Wireframing

With some wireframes and all the content on hand, I began coding using Atom.

I started my design process with low-fidelity wireframes, creating a foundational structure and functionality. When creating mid-fidelity wireframes, I refined some layouts while maintaining flexibility for adjustments based on user feedback, finally creating high-fidelity wireframes that detail the final product's design, features, and interactions.

Testing and Iterations

In preparation for refining the Mentor Me application, I created a usability test plan to assess its current state. The plan aimed to evaluate the prototype's functionality and user experience through a series of 3 structured tasks with 6 participants. Using the Jakob Nielsen’s error severity rating scale, I was able to find usability issues to create some improvements.

In the comprehensive usability testing of Mentor Me's mobile and desktop applications, participants reported an overall positive experience. While the overall feedback was favorable I still found some specific areas for enhancement to refine the user experience.

Usability Test Results

  • Add labels for mentor profiles on the mobile side to improve clarity and navigation.
  • Expand the range of categories and topics on the resources page for a more comprehensive selection of content.
  • Implement a prompt for users to message mentors seamlessly, enhancing interaction dynamics.
  • Offer an additional option for users to schedule calls directly on the mentor's profile to optimize the booking process.

Takeaways

In this case study, I explore the concept of continuously enhancing design. It's about the ongoing process of refining and perfecting user experiences. I share my journey from research to prototyping, emphasizing the significance of perpetual learning and evolution. By embracing feedback and maintaining flexibility, I illustrate how design is a continual journey of growth and improvement.
